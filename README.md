Deep Analytical Insights:

May represents a near-total collapse in profitability — a 97.9% plunge from March. This is not a seasonal dip; it is a red-flag event.
Possible root causes:
Major supply-chain disruption (stock-out of high-margin items)
Sudden increase in COGS or aggressive discounting
External factors (economic policy change, competitor campaign, or regional lockdown in key cities)
Data error or incomplete May reporting (but the dashboard shows it as final)
Business impact: If May’s low performance repeats, annual profit could drop by over 20%. Immediate investigation required.

Extremely balanced distribution — no single city exceeds 26%. This indicates healthy nationwide coverage and no over-reliance on one market.

Kano (25%) is surprisingly strong despite being northern — suggests successful expansion into non-coastal markets.
Opportunity: Port Harcourt (lowest COGS) could be under-invested. Increase marketing or sales rep allocation here to balance the portfolio.
